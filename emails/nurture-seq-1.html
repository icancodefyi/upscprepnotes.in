Here's something most UPSC aspirants get wrong about answer writing — it's not about writing more, it's about structuring better.

We analysed 50+ topper answer sheets and found a clear pattern. The top scorers (140+) all follow a consistent structure:

1. Introduction (2-3 lines)
Define the keyword, give context, state your stance.
2. Body (4-6 points)
Use headings, diagrams, and underlining. One idea per paragraph.
3. Conclusion (2-3 lines)
Summarize, add a forward-looking statement, link to current affairs.

The best way to learn this structure? Study actual topper answer sheets. See exactly how they used headings, diagrams, and underlining to score 140+ in every paper.
