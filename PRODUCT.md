# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Serious UPSC aspirants actively preparing for the Civil Services Examination. They are data-driven, evidence-based learners who want to study what toppers actually did — not marketing promises. Context: researching topper strategies, comparing marks across optional subjects, downloading answer copies, evaluating test series. Often on mobile (67%), comparing multiple toppers, looking for patterns in marks data. Secondary users: coaching institutes evaluating their test series placement, and casual browsers discovering topper profiles through search.

## Product Purpose

India's largest repository of verified UPSC topper data. The site exists to democratize access to topper marksheets, answer copies, strategy breakdowns, and optional subject analysis that was previously scattered across Telegram groups and coaching centers. Success = a serious aspirant can make a data-backed decision about their optional subject, answer writing strategy, and test series choice within 10 minutes of landing on the site.

## Brand Personality

Authoritative, trustworthy, student-friendly. Voice: like a senior IAS officer who remembers what prep felt like — knowledgeable but not condescending, direct but not cold. Tone: evidence over opinion, data over promises, clarity over hype. Emotional goal: confidence that this is the one place with real data, not recycled coaching sales pitches.

## Anti-references

- **Unacademy / Byju's / PW style**: No flashy gradients, no "AI-powered" badges, no gamification (streaks, leaderboards), no aggressive upsell popups, no countdown timers, no "only 3 left!" urgency. No neon accent colors or dark mode by default.
- **Generic SaaS dashboard**: No cream/navy palettes, no identical card grids with icon + heading + text repeated 6 times, no tiny uppercase tracked eyebrows above every section, no numbered section markers (01/02/03) as scaffolding, no gradient text, no glassmorphism.
- **Ed-tech landing page cliches**: No "Trusted by 10L+ students" banners, no floating WhatsApp widgets, no auto-playing video heroes, no "Download Free PDF" popups on every page.

## Design Principles

1. **Data speaks first** — Let topper marks, rank numbers, and answer copies be the visual hero. Numbers carry authority; don't bury them in paragraphs.
2. **Earn the scroll** — Every section must answer "why should I keep scrolling?" If a section doesn't advance the aspirant's decision, cut it.
3. **Evidence over promises** — Show methodology, show sources, show real marksheets. The site's credibility IS the design.
4. **Respect the student's time** — 56% mobile, probably studying between classes. Fast loads, clear navigation, no decorative detours.
5. **Authoritative without intimidation** — Serious data presented with warmth. The design should feel like a well-organized library, not a courtroom.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG 2.1 AA compliance as baseline
- Mobile-first responsive design (67% of traffic)
- Hindi content support for key pages (4 guides available in Hindi)
- Keyboard navigable for all interactive elements
- High contrast text (near-black on near-white) for readability in various lighting conditions
- Reduced motion support via prefers-reduced-motion
