I Analyzed 238 UPSC Topper Marksheets — Here's Which Paper Actually Decides Your Rank

The 27-mark gap between GS3 and GS4 that most aspirants ignore

---

Every UPSC aspirant has a "weak paper." But when I sat down with 238 real topper marksheets and crunched the numbers, one thing became painfully clear: almost everyone struggles with the same paper.

I run **UPSCPrepNotes** — a repository of verified topper marksheets, answer copies, and strategy breakdowns from 280+ real rank holders. We pulled every marksheet we had and calculated the average score across each paper.

Here's what the data says.

---

The Averages (across 238 toppers)

| Paper | Average Score |
|---|---|
| GS1 (History, Geography, Culture) | 97 |
| GS2 (Polity, Governance, IR) | 112 |
| GS3 (Economy, S&T, Environment, Security) | 88 |

GS3 averages **88 marks**. That's **27 marks lower** than GS4 and Essay.

Think about that for a second. GS4 — the paper everyone panics about because it's "subjective" and "unpredictable" — averages 115. GS3 — Economy, Science & Technology, Environment, Disaster Management, Internal Security — averages 88.

The paper most people fear (Ethics) is actually where toppers score highest. The paper most people neglect (GS3) is where ranks are made and lost.

---

The GS3 Problem

Only **25 toppers** in our database of 238 scored 100+ in GS3. That's roughly **10%**.

Meanwhile, the same group of toppers scored 140+ in GS4 — a paper with a lower maximum — four times.

| Topper | GS4 Score | AIR | Year |

| Aditya Srivastava | 143 | 1 | 2023 |
| Garima Lohia | 141 | 2 | 2022 |

Why this gap? Three reasons the data reveals:

**1. GS3 is a hybrid paper.** It tests both static (Economy, Environment) and dynamic (S&T, Security) topics. Most aspirants prepare them separately and fail to connect them in answers.

**2. Economy drags everyone down.** Even top rankers score lower in Economy-related questions. The average topper loses 5-8 marks here compared to their other papers.

**3. S&T feels infinite.** Science & Technology is the only UPSC paper with no fixed syllabus boundary. Aspirants prepare less because they don't know where to stop.

The Real Takeaway

If you're preparing for UPSC 2026 or 2027, here's what the data tells you to prioritize:

| Paper | Avg Score | Verdict |
|---|---|---|
| GS4 (Ethics) | 115 | Don't over-invest. Easy 110+. |
| Essay | 115 | Safety net. Consistent practice. |
| GS2 (Polity, IR) | 112 | Safety net. Won't make your rank but protects it. |
| GS1 (History, Culture) | 97 | Differentiator at the top. AIR 1s score 110+. |
| GS3 (Economy, S&T, Security) | 88 | Biggest leverage point. Every extra mark = 10-15 ranks. |

**Don't over-invest in Ethics.** GS4 averages 115 and is the most forgiving paper. A good strategy + basic case studies = 110+. The marginal return on extra Ethics prep is low.

**Invest in GS3.** That 27-mark gap is the single biggest leverage point in your preparation. Every extra mark in GS3 moves you up 10-15 ranks.

**GS2 and Essay are your safety nets.** They average 112 and 115 respectively. Consistent practice = consistent marks. These papers won't make your rank, but they'll protect it.

**GS1 is a differentiator at the top.** The average is 97, but AIR 1s score 110+. History and Culture are high-reward areas.
